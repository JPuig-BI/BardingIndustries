The Strategic Challenge

What are REEs, and why do they matter?

Rare Earth Elements are 17 critical minerals found in virtually every advanced technology we depend on: from defense systems and electric vehicles to smartphones and wind turbines. Without them, modern civilization stops.

  • A single F-35 fighter jet requires over 400 kg of rare earth materials
  • Every EV motor relies on neodymium magnets, a rare earth product
  • Wind turbines, MRI machines, and consumer electronics all depend on REE supply chains
  • The global REE market is projected to exceed $20B by 2030
~85%

Of global rare earth production is controlled by a single strategic competitor, creating a critical chokepoint in Western supply chains.

17

Elements classified as rare earths, each with unique magnetic, optical, and catalytic properties that cannot be easily substituted.

The Cliban Platform

A biological solution
to a geopolitical problem

Our proprietary bioleaching technology processes low-grade and secondary feedstocks that conventional methods cannot, unlocking REE sources previously considered uneconomical.

Grade-Agnostic Secondary Sources Scalable Process Defense Supply Chain
.01

Feedstock

Mine tailings, electronic waste, and low-grade ores that conventional hydrometallurgy cannot economically process. The untapped reserves hiding in plain sight.

.02

Extraction

Proprietary microorganisms mobilise rare earth elements from the host matrix through targeted bioleaching reactions. No harsh solvents, no high-temperature processing.

.03

Separation

Selective biological mechanisms isolate individual REEs, a process that typically requires expensive, multi-stage solvent extraction cascades to achieve conventionally.

.04

Product

High-purity rare earth compounds delivered into defence, clean energy, and electronics supply chains from sources once considered off-limits.
