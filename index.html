The Strategic Challenge

Rare earths power the modern world. A strategic competitor controls the supply.

>80% U.S. reliance on foreign rare earth processing
>95% of global REE supply controlled by a single strategic competitor
12 of 17 rare earth elements already weaponized by a strategic competitor

A strategic competitor controls what the West can buy.

A strategic competitor has already restricted rare earth exports as a geopolitical lever, cutting supply to Western manufacturers and defense contractors without warning or recourse.

7x projected increase in global REE demand by 2040 across AI, clean energy, and defense
2027 DFARS mandate: U.S.-origin sourcing required for defense

The minerals are already here, but wasted.

The bottleneck is processing, not geology. Conventional methods cannot handle low-grade feedstocks, so most of what the U.S. extracts is discarded as waste before it ever reaches a supply chain.

51 kt REO extracted in the U.S. annually
9 kt actually purified
20–30 kt discarded as unprocessable waste every year
